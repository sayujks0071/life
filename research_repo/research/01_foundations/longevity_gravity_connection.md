# Longevity-Gravity-Counter-Curvature Connection

## Research Question
How does the sitting-rising test (SRT) in Japanese populations relate to:
1. Gravitational mechanotransduction
2. Longevity genes/proteins
3. Biological counter-curvature / information-elasticity framework

## Key Longevity Genes & Proteins to Investigate

### Mechanosensitive Longevity Pathways
1. **FOXO3** - Master longevity regulator, mechanosensitive
2. **SIRT1** - NAD+ dependent deacetylase, responds to mechanical stress
3. **mTOR** - Mechanistic target of rapamycin, nutrient/mechanical sensing
4. **AMPK** - Energy sensor, activated by muscle contraction
5. **Klotho** - Anti-aging protein, involved in calcium/phosphate homeostasis

### Muscle-Specific Longevity Proteins
6. **PGC-1α** - Mitochondrial biogenesis, exercise response
7. **Myostatin (GDF8)** - Muscle growth inhibitor
8. **IGF-1** - Insulin-like growth factor, muscle maintenance

### Mechanotransduction Proteins
9. **YAP/TAZ** - Hippo pathway, mechanical force sensors
10. **Integrins** - ECM-cytoskeleton linkers, force transmission

## Hypothesis: Gravity-Longevity-Counter-Curvature Link

### Sitting-Rising Test as Gravitational Challenge
- **Mechanical Load**: Repeated transitions against gravity
- **Muscle Activation**: Eccentric/concentric contractions
- **Balance/Proprioception**: Spinal curvature adjustments
- **Information Flow**: Mechanosensors → gene expression

### Counter-Curvature Interpretation
1. **Spinal Geometry**: SRT requires dynamic spinal curvature changes
2. **Information Input**: Gravitational field provides constant "information"
3. **Elastic Response**: Muscle-tendon systems store/release energy
4. **Longevity Output**: Mechanotransduction activates longevity pathways

### Proposed Mechanism
```
Gravitational Challenge (SRT)
    ↓
Spinal Counter-Curvature Dynamics
    ↓
Mechanotransduction (Integrins, YAP/TAZ)
    ↓
```
