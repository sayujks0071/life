# Literature Map: Biological Counter-Curvature

This map organizes our research by conceptual theme, illustrating the taxonomy of the Biological Counter-Curvature (BCC) theory.

## 1. Information-Elasticity Coupling (IEC)

*Explores how developmental identities ($I$) modify mechanical properties ($E$ or $\kappa_{rest}$).*

- **Why it matters**: Bridges the gap between genetic patterning and macro-scale geometry.
- **Canonical Readings**:
  - [BCC Theory Draft](../research/01_foundations/draft_theory_BCC.md)
  - [Moulton et al. (2013) - Morphoelastic rods](references.bib)

## 2. Spinal Biomechanics & Cosserat Rods

*Mathematical modeling of the spine as a slender elastic structure.*

- **Why it matters**: Provides the physical constraints within which biological information must operate.
- **Canonical Readings**:
  - [Gazzola et al. (2018) - Forward and inverse problems](references.bib)
  - [White & Panjabi (1990) - Clinical Biomechanics](references.bib)

## 3. Evolutionary Context: Gravity & Longevity

*How the S-shaped spine adapts to earth's gravity and influences healthspan.*

- **Why it matters**: Connects geometry to physiological outcomes and evolutionary fitness.
- **Canonical Readings**:
  - [Longevity & Gravity Connection](../research/01_foundations/longevity_gravity_connection.md)
  - [Green & Scott (2018) - Spinal changes in microgravity](references.bib)

## 4. Pathophysiology: Scoliosis & Bifurcation

*When the counter-curvature mechanism fails or encounters instabilities.*

- **Why it matters**: Testing the theory by predicting and explaining clinical pathologies.
- **Canonical Readings**:
  - [Weinstein et al. (2008) - Adolescent idiopathic scoliosis](references.bib)
  - [Grimes et al. (2016) - Zebrafish models](references.bib)
